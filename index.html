THE FILM PRODUCTION COMPANIES

Major film studios are production and distribution companies that release a number of films annually and command a significant share of box office revenue. Today, the Big Three majors – Universal Pictures, Warner Bros. Pictures, and Columbia Pictures – dominated the global film industry and made approximately 45 billions in the box office history.

Being a leader in film entertainment, Universal Pictures has released more than 300 titles, including classics such as Fast & Furious, Despicable Me, Jurassic World, Jaws and etc. In 2015, the highest-grossing movie created by Universal to date brought in $652 million domestically and a crazy $1.6 billion worldwide.

Columbia Pictures is an American film production studio that is a member of the Sony Pictures Motion Picture Group, founded in 1918. The Columbia Pictures becomes one of the major film companies in the World with the its popular titles: Ghostbusters II (1989), Spider-Man(2002), 21 Jump Street(2012), Venom(2018), Jumanji:The Next Level(2019).

Warner Bros. Pictures is an American film production and distribution company founded in 1923 by brothers Harry Warner, Albert Warner, Sam Warner, and Jack L. Warner. In 2011, the company produced a movie, Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows – Part 2 which grossed total of $1,342,222,791 worldwide. It is the highest-grossing film in the Harry Potter franchise.
